* 450g golden caster sugar
* 300ml single cream
* 100g salted butter
* 2 tablespoons of vanilla extract

# Implements

* Baking tray/tin (the one I have is about A4-sized, and about a centimetre deep)
* Baking/greaseproof paper
* A mixing implement
* A saucepan
* A sharp knife or a pizza cutter
* A set of scales
* A kitchen thermometer (the long metal probe kind)

# Instructions

* Chop the butter into cubes

* Place all ingredients except the vanilla in the saucepan 

* Melt together on a low-medium heat, stir regularly.

* Once it's all mixed to an even consistency and all the butter has melted.

* Turn the heat to medium-high and bring it to boil.

* Keep boiling until the mixture reaches 116°C, then remove from the heat.

* Let it cool to about 110°C, then add the vanilla extract

* Beat the mixture until the mixture thickens and becomes harder to beat.

* Tip the mixture into a backing tray lined with baking paper, and level it out as best as possible

* Leave to set

* Slice using the pizza cutter.

# Notes

The final texture is controlled by how hot the mixture is allowed to boil - too low and the mixture will won't set properly, too high and it will become grainy, and eventually biscuity.

If the mixture is left to boil for too long, it will turn into something more like toffee. Which is still quite nice, but not the point of making fudge.
